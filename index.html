Fat Dot

Fat Dot is a game I made by myself, where players try to chain explosions to reach a high score.

There are 6 different enemies, designed to complement each other and encourage the player to move around in different ways. To help with the design, I programmed some debug abilities into the game.

The debug build hot-reloads newly compiled code, and any changes will update while the game is still running. The game can be rebalanced in real-time without having to quit and restart with every change.

The debug build can also record and replay gameplay in real-time. Used in conjunction with hot-reload, this allowed me to record gameplay, modify settings, then replay the recording to see changes in action. Control can be returned at any point during the replay, and play will continue from there.

I used C++ to make the game, SDL 2.0 for cross-platform compatibility, and OpenGL for rendering. The game is playable on Android, iOS, Windows, Linux, and Chrome/Firefox.

DekoBoko

DekoBoko is a fan game paying tribute the movie, My Neighbor Totoro. Gameplay is split into two parts. In the first part, players tend to their own garden. In the second part, players control the small Totoro to grow a giant tree, recreating an iconic scene from the film.

Like with Fat Dot, I developed the game solo. The art and animations are all hand drawn. Players can discover 17 different plants and 19 different animals, each with their own entry in the in-game journal.

The music is a self-arranged piano cover of the movie's score. I recorded the song in 6 layers, so the game could fade in the layers separately. Players can water their garden to background violin chords. When they switch to the tree-growing game, a piano melody fades in to match the ascending action.

I used Unity3D to make the game, and is available on Android and iOS.

Shepherd and Dog

Shepherd and Dog is a game made in 3 days for the Ludum Dare Game Jam. Players control a shepherd-and-dog team to guide their sheep back home.

I did the programming and game design, and teamed up with an artist to complete the art assets. My partner and I made the levels together. Since I didn't use an engine, I instead created a basic, level editor for my teammate to use. The editor used simple ASCII text, and we were able to make 11 levels before the deadline.

I used C++ to make the game, and Box2D for the physics. The game is available on Windows and Chrome/Firefox.

Asteroids But With Gravity

Asteroids But With Gravity is an Asteroids clone with a gravity gimmick. Players must shoot asteroids in the classic arcade style, while escaping the gravity of nearby planets.

Normally, objects loops around the screen in Asteroids, but I wanted a bigger playing field. In Asteroids But With Gravity, the screen follows the player, but the environment loops around for the same effect but without the appearance of abrubtly teleporting.

I made the game using Unreal Engine 4, and Blender for the art assets. The game is available on Windows.

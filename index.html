Fat Dot

Fat Dot is a high-score game where you try to create a long, chain-reaction of explosions.

I made everything in the game by myself using C++, and used SDL 2.0 for cross-platform compatibility and OpenGL for rendering. The game is playable on Android, iOS, Windows, Linux, and Chrome and Firefox.

There are 6 enemy types, and I designed their behavior to play off each other and encourage the player to move around in different ways. To help with the design, I programmed some debug abilities into the game.

The debug build can hot-reload newly compiled code, so any changes made to the game will update while the game is running. This way, I could balance the game without having to quit and restart every time I changed something.

The debug build also recorded the gameplay, and could rewind and replay what happened in realtime. This, in conjunction with hot-reload, allowed me to record myself play the game, then watch what would happen when I changed different settings. Control can also be returned at any point during the replay, and play will continue from there.

DekoBoko

DekoBoko is a fan-game that references a scene in the movie, My Neighbor Totoro. The gameplay is split into two parts, with first part dedicated to gardening and the second part recreating the movie scene. I used Unity3D to make this game, and is available on Android and iOS.

I drew all the art and animations. I made 17 different plants and 19 different animals to discover. Each plant and animal also has an entry in the in-game journal.

The music is my piano cover of the movie's score. I recorded it in separate parts, playing along to myself, so I could fade in the parts separately. For example, I fade in the melody line during action, and just play chords in the garden.

Shepherd and Dog

Shepherd and Dog is a game made for the Ludum Dare game jam in 3 days. In it, you control the two characters at the same time to guide sheep to the end of a level.

I teamed up with an artist to make this game. I did the programming and game design. The game is available on Windows and Chrome and Firefox.

My teammate and I made the levels together. But since I didn't use an engine, I had to create a basic level-editor for my teammate to use. It was a simple editor that used ASCII text, but we were able to make 11 levels by the deadline.

Asteroids But With Gravity

As the title states, this is an Asteroids clone with a gravity gimmick. I made the game using Unreal Engine 4, and Blender for the art assets.

Normally, stuff loops around the screen in Asteroids, but I wanted a bigger playing field. Instead, the screen follows the player, but the game world still loops around without the appearance of teleporting.
